# Foundry Voice Guide — Craft First

> **Draft · PASS-032 companion**  
> Foundry helps people become who they are capable of becoming — but we say it through **craft, proof, and guild language**, not self-help slogans.

---

## North Star

**Write like a serious hobbyist would talk to another serious hobbyist.**

Not: *Improve yourself.*  
Yes: *Dial in your opening. Log the game. Teach the fork.*

The product is transformation. The voice is **mastery culture**.

---

## The Share Test

Before shipping copy, ask:

> Would someone who cares about this domain share a screenshot without cringing?

If the headline sounds like a wellness app, a LinkedIn post, or a course funnel — rewrite it.

---

## Voice Layers

Foundry has four tone layers. Same architecture; different vocabulary.

| Layer | Audience | Tone | Example headline |
|-------|----------|------|------------------|
| **Platform** | Everyone landing on Foundry | Proof, infrastructure, long arc | *Every path has a community. Every contribution helps someone else master the craft.* |
| **Life Leverage** | Parents, students, career pivots | Outcome + agency, not therapy | *Become future-proof — ship projects, not just watch tutorials.* |
| **Passion Trinity** | Decades-long identity domains | Role titles, ritual, craft | *Become a Pitmaster · Become a Bourbon Steward* |
| **Skills Guild** | Chess, poker, physics, gardening | Rank, study, club, tournament | *Beginner → Master · Rated play · Post-mortems · Teach the fork* |

**Rule:** Life Leverage may say *become*. Passion and Skills say **what role you hold at the board, pit, or shelf**.

## Mission Loop — Default Labels

Current UI loop:

```txt
Mission → Build → Show → Reflect → Improve → Mentor
```

**Recommended default (Skills + Passion):**

```txt
Mission → Build → Show → Debrief → Refine → Teach
```

| Phase | Avoid | Prefer | When to vary |
|-------|-------|--------|--------------|
| Mission | *Set your intention* | *Pick the line, cook, or puzzle* | Life Leverage: *Define the outcome* |
| Build | *Work on yourself* | *Play, smoke, ship, solve* | — |
| Show | *Celebrate growth* | *Log the game, share the hand, capture evidence* | — |
| Reflect | *How did you grow?* | **Debrief** · *What did you miss?* | Civic: *What changed in the room?* |
| Improve | *Become better* | **Refine** · *One adjustment* | BBQ: *Dial in temps* · Bourbon: *Adjust your palate note* |
| Mentor | *Give back* | **Teach** · *Walk someone through it* | — |

Chess World implements this loop first. Other worlds migrate in copy passes — not all at once.

## Words We Use / Words We Avoid

### Use

- craft, mastery, study, repertoire, evidence, post-mortem, club, rated, steward, pitmaster, host, analyst
- become **[role]** — *Become a Tactician*, not *Become your best self*
- path, guild, circle, society (community names)
- log, shelf, journal, portfolio (proof artifacts)

### Avoid (unless quoting user input)

- improve yourself, self-improvement, unlock your potential, journey of growth
- mindset shift, level up your life, transform your mindset
- hustle, grind culture (unless domain-native, e.g. poker bankroll)
- guru, life coach, wellness

### Platform exception

Homepage may keep the human OS line — *Help me become the person I want to be* — as **private intent**, not marketing headline. Public surfaces lead with **paths and craft**.

## Passion Trinity — Frame Lock

```txt
Bourbon  → Appreciate Craft
BBQ      → Create Experiences
Poker    → Strategic Thinking
```

Outcome lines are **roles**, not vibes:

- *Become a Bourbon Steward* — not *Become a better you*
- *Become a Pitmaster* — not *Level up your cooking*
- *Become a Strategic Player* — not *Master decision-making in life*

## Skills Guild — Chess as Exemplar

Chess is the template for **Skills Guild** voice: rank, study, club, proof.

### Frame

```txt
Chess → Think Deeply
Outcome: Beginner → Master
Community: Chess Study Circle
```

### Level ladder (consumer-facing)

| Level | Title | Tagline |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Beginner | Rules, mates, and your first complete games |
| 2 | Novice | Tactics habits and notation |
| 3 | Club Player | Regular play with a repertoire sketch |
| 4 | Tactician | Patterns, combinations, calculation |
| 5 | Strategist | Plans, structures, middlegame judgment |
| 6 | Competitor | Rated games, clocks, tournament prep |
| 7 | Candidate | Deep study plan and norm-track milestones |
| 8 | Master | Teach, lead study, publish your standard |

### Chess-specific vocabulary

- **Post-mortem** — not *reflection on your journey*
- **Repertoire** — not *learning path*
- **Rating** / **norm** — proof, not badges
- **Study circle** — not *support group*
- **Blunder** / **inaccuracy** — honest debrief language

### Sample mission subtitles (voice-approved)

- *Treat every game like evidence — not entertainment.*
- *Forks and pins until you see them without thinking.*
- *One opening for White, one for Black — depth beats breadth.*
- *King and pawn endings decide more games than you think.*
- *Your first rated game is a baseline, not a verdict.*

## Domain Variant Cheat Sheet

When **Refine** feels wrong, use domain-native verbs:

| Domain | Refine variant | Teach variant |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| Bourbon | *Adjust your tasting note* | *Host someone’s first pour* |
| BBQ | *Dial in temps* | *Walk them through their first fire* |
| Poker | *Patch one leak* | *Review a beginner’s hand* |
| Chess | *Add one line to your repertoire* | *Explain a fork on the board* |
| Civic | *Fix one process* | *Show a neighbor how to register* |
| AI Builder | *Tighten the prompt* | *Pair with a classmate on their build* |

## Copy Checklist (every new world)

1. **Outcome** — role title, not self-help (`Become a Master Gardener`)
2. **Frame** — one verb phrase (`Think Deeply`, `Appreciate Craft`)
3. **Mission loop** — Debrief / Refine / Teach where possible
4. **Evidence** — what gets logged (game PGN, smoke log, bankroll sheet)
5. **Community** — guild name (`Chess Study Circle`, not `Chess Improvement Group`)
6. **Share test** — would a club player post this?
